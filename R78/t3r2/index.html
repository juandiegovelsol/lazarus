About the Plants

Plants are essential to life on Earth, providing oxygen and beauty. Whether you're growing sunflowers, dahlias, or other vibrant plants, it's crucial to know their care requirements for healthy growth.

Care Instructions

Sunflower

Watering: Sunflowers need regular watering but ensure the soil isn't soggy. Aim to keep the soil moist.

Light: They thrive in full sunlight.

Dahlia

Watering: Dahlias need deep watering once or twice a week.

Light: Provide at least 6 hours of sunlight for best growth.

Cactus

Watering: Water sparingly and allow the soil to dry out completely between waterings.

Light: Cacti thrive in bright, indirect sunlight.

Tropical Plant

Watering: These plants prefer consistently moist soil, but not waterlogged.

Light: Partial shade or indirect sunlight is best.

Succulent

Watering: Water sparingly, about once every two weeks. Ensure good drainage.

Light: Bright, indirect sunlight is ideal for succulents.

Fern

Watering: Keep the soil consistently moist, but avoid overwatering.

Light: Ferns do well in partial to full shade.

Monstera

Watering: Water every 1-2 weeks, allowing the soil to dry out between waterings.

Light: Bright, indirect sunlight is perfect for Monsteras.

Orchid

Watering: Water about once a week, ensuring proper drainage to avoid root rot.

Light: Orchids require indirect sunlight for healthy growth.

Lavender

Watering: Water once the top inch of soil feels dry.

Light: Full sun is necessary for growing lavender.
